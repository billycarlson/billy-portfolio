<h2>Choosing vision before optimization</h2>

<p>
Rather than starting with incremental tweaks, I proposed a different approach:
create a future-state desktop homepage that clarified where we were going before
deciding how far we could realistically move in the near term.
</p>

<p>
This reframing helped shift conversations away from individual modules and toward
overall structure, hierarchy, and intent.
</p>

<p>
The goal wasnâ€™t to ship everything immediately. It was to create a north star that
teams could align around.
</p>

<h2>Designing for alignment, not just approval</h2>

<p>
The design work focused on:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Clarifying visual hierarchy across content types</li>
  <li>Reducing cognitive load by grouping related information</li>
  <li>Establishing more consistent patterns for promotions and discovery</li>
  <li>Making brand expression feel intentional rather than decorative</li>
</ul>

<p>
Just as important as the design itself was how it was presented. I treated reviews
as alignment moments, not just checkpoints.

<h2>Navigating tradeoffs without losing trust</h2>

<p>
Not everything in the future vision could ship immediately. Technical constraints,
dependencies with mobile teams, and ongoing experiments required prioritization.
</p>

<p>
Instead of defending every detail, I focused on explaining tradeoffs clearly:
what mattered now, what could wait, and why.
</p>

<p>
This transparency helped maintain momentum while avoiding false expectations.
</p>
