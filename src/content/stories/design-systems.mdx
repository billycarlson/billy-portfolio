<section id="context">
  <div class="label">Context</div>
  <h2>Growth exposes inconsistency</h2>

  <p>
    As organizations scale, design inconsistencies become more visible. Teams move
    fast, but without shared standards, products begin to fragment.
  </p>
</section>

<section id="challenge">
  <div class="label">The Challenge</div>
  <h2>Adoption is harder than creation</h2>

  <p>
    Building components is relatively easy. Getting teams to trust and use them
    consistently is not.
  </p>

  <p>
    The real challenge is aligning incentives, workflows, and expectations across
    disciplines.
  </p>
</section>

<section id="approach">
  <div class="label">Approach</div>
  <h2>Design systems as shared language</h2>

  <p>
    I treat design systems as organizational infrastructure â€” something that enables
    better decisions, not just faster production.
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>Focus on clarity over completeness</li>
    <li>Involve engineering early</li>
    <li>Anchor standards to real product needs</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="impact">
  <div class="label">Impact</div>
  <h2>Consistency without rigidity</h2>

  <p>
    When design systems are positioned as tools rather than rules, adoption improves.
    Teams move faster with fewer debates and better outcomes.
  </p>
</section>

<section id="reflection">
  <div class="label">Reflection</div>
  <h2>Systems succeed when people believe in them</h2>

  <p>
    The success of a design system depends less on its documentation and more on the
    relationships that support it.
  </p>
</section>
